The Problem & The Fix

Why Your Tankless Needs Annual Flushing

Orange County's hard water is among the toughest in the nation. Here's what's happening inside your system right now — and what a flush does about it.

Without Annual Flushing

Hard water minerals (calcium, magnesium) accumulate inside the heat exchanger, creating a thick layer of scale that gets worse every year.

  • Reduced hot water flow & pressure
  • Higher energy bills from lost efficiency
  • Strange popping or rumbling noises
  • Manufacturer warranty voided
  • Heat exchanger failure — up to $1,500 repair
  • Premature system replacement

With Annual Flushing

A professional descaling flush clears the buildup before it causes damage — restoring performance and protecting your investment year after year.

  • Hot water flow & pressure fully restored
  • Energy efficiency back to factory levels
  • Quiet, reliable operation
  • Warranty requirements met & documented
  • Heat exchanger protected from failure
  • System lifespan extended up to 20 years

Common Questions

Tankless Flush FAQs

How do I know if my tankless needs flushing?

Common signs include lukewarm water that used to be hot, longer wait times at the tap, popping or rumbling noises from the unit, reduced flow rate, error codes on the display, or visible mineral buildup around the unit's valves. In Orange County, if it's been more than 12 months since your last flush, you're due regardless of symptoms.

How often should I flush my tankless water heater?

Annually, at minimum. Orange County has some of the hardest water in the country, so some homes — especially newer developments without whole-house filtration — benefit from a flush every 6–9 months. Manufacturers like Rinnai, Navien, and Noritz require annual flushing as a condition of warranty coverage.

My unit is only a year or two old — do I really need this?

Yes. Manufacturer warranties on Rinnai, Navien, and Noritz require annual flushing starting from year one. Skipping the first flush can void warranty coverage and start scale buildup that compounds every year.

What does the $125 flush include?

Full system inspection, professional descaling flush of the heat exchanger, inlet filter cleaning, supply line flush, performance testing, and detailed service documentation. Everything needed to keep your unit running efficiently and your warranty valid. No upsells.

How long does a flush take?

About 1 to 1.5 hours, including setup, the 45-minute descaling circulation, and post-flush testing.

Can I flush my tankless myself?

You can. You'll need a flush kit ($150–$200), descaling solution, and roughly two hours. For most homeowners, the cost difference compared to a professional flush is small — and a botched DIY can void manufacturer warranty if you can't document the maintenance properly.
